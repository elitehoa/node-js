Apollo Lesson Plan

Warm up/Review Game
10
Reader Page 1 - A Before Reading
0
Stage aim
  • Before Reading sets up the context of the story by engaging students with visual clues and guided questions, which predict the language they will see and hear.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate replies and neat handwriting for questions 1 and 2, active participation for question 3 which they may answer in their shared L1.
Reader Page 1 - B. While Reading 1.a Listen and follow along in your storybook
0
Stage aim
  • While Reading tasks engage students at a deeper level by giving them a purpose to read. Listen and follow along in your storybook ensures that students are exposed to the natural rhythms, pronunciation and intonation of the language aurally while they read.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active listening and students following the story in their books with their fingers in synchronicity with the audio, with all students engaged and on-task.
Reader Page 1 - 1.b Listen and echo
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and echo provides a model for natural intonation and authentic pronunciation of the language in the story, which students then produce orally themselves.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic echoing of the language (pronunciation and intonation) and active participation from all students.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read together
0
Stage aim
  • Read together practises oral production of the language in the story through chorus reading aloud.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct pronunciation and authentic intonation and active participation from all students.
Reader Page 1 - 3. Role-play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • Role-play in groups provides an opportunity for freer, less-scaffolded production of the language in the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic and confident production of the language that conveys an understanding of meaning and all students participating actively.
Reader Page 1 - 4. Complete the worksheet
0
Stage aim
  • Complete the worksheet checks students’ comprehension of the story and vocabulary through a series of multiple-choice questions.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct answers, neat circling and all students being on-task.

0
Stage aim
  • After Reading allows students to continue working with the language of the story whilst developing other process skills. In the Role-play in groups these skills include performing and turn taking.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic and confident production of the language that conveys an understanding of meaning and all students participating actively.
Reader Page 2 - 2. Complete the story map in pairs
0
Stage aim
  • Complete the story map in pairs works on categorising, sequencing and communication skills.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct and logical mapping that conveys understanding of the language.
