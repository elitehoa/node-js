Apollo Lesson Plan

Warm up/Review Game
10
Reader Page 1 - A Review 1, Listen and follow along in your storybook.
0
Stage aim
  • Review reinforces understanding of the full story by engaging students in listening and text tracking to recall key events and language. Listen and follow along in your storybook provides a listening model and purpose for reading by allowing students to hear natural rhythm, stress, and pronunciation while tracking the text.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for focused listening, students following along in their books, and correct tracking with fingers.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read together
0
Stage aim
  • Read together practises oral production and fluency through shared reading, supporting confidence and reinforcing correct rhythm and stress patterns.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct pronunciation, appropriate intonation, and whole-group participation.
Reader Page 1 - 3. Role-play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • Role-play in groups is an opportunity for freer, less-scaffolded language use, encouraging confidence and expressive speaking through character interaction.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic and confident production of the language, turn-taking, and active participation.
Reader Page 1 - 4. Read the sentences and choose True or False.
0
Stage aim
  • Read the sentences and choose True or False checks students’ understanding of story details and endings through True or False questions, requiring careful reading and accurate judgment.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct True/False choices, careful reading, and students staying on task.
Break
0
Reader Page 2 - B. Practice 1. Work in pairs. Complete the story map.
0
Stage aim
  • Practice allows students to understand the story better by organizing key information. Complete the story map in pairs checks students’ understanding of story structure, characters, setting, and key events by organizing information from the text into a beginning–middle–ending framework.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate completion of details, correct use of vocabulary from the word bank, cooperative pair work, and students staying on task.
Reader Page 2 - Sing along!
0
Stage aim
  • Sing along reinforces comprehension and oral fluency through rhythmic repetition, making language memorable and engaging.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident singing, clear pronunciation, and enthusiastic participation.
