Apollo Lesson Plan

Warm up/Review Game
10
Reader Page 1 - A Review 1. Listen and follow along in your storybook.
0
Stage aim
  • Review allows students to listen and read the story again, checking comprehension and providing a model. Listen and follow along in your storybook re-familiarises students with the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active listening and students following the story in their books with their fingers.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read together.
0
Stage aim
  • Read together practises oral production of the language in the story through chorus reading.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct pronunciation and authentic intonation, and active participation.
Reader Page 1 - 3. Role-play in groups.
0
Stage aim
  • Role play in groups provides students with the opportunity to perform together and produce the language of the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident production of the language that conveys an understanding of meaning, turn taking and active participation.
Reader Page 1 - 4. Read the sentences and decide who did the actions.
0
Stage aim
  • Read and decide checks students’ comprehension of the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct choices which students can justify, and neat circling.
Break
0
Reader Page 2 - B. Practise 1. Work in pairs. Complete the story map.
0
Stage aim
  • Practise provides students with further comprehension practice of the story. Complete the story map works on comprehension, categorisation and summarising skills while discussing answers with one another.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct answers, neat and accurate handwriting and book work.
Reader Page 2 - Sing Along!
0
Stage aim
  • Sing along further practises oral production of the story and reinforces comprehension.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident singing, correct pronunciation, and active participation.
