Student Book p.45 - A Listen and number
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and number checks students' recall of the target vocabulary from Units 3 and 4 (fruit and vegetables and animals), and gives them the opportunity to practise listening for detail.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful listening, accurate identification of the target vocabulary and correct numbering of the boxes.
Context
  • “Let’s go to the zoo today. We can see the animals and find out what food they like.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.46 - C. Listen and read
15
Stage aim
  • Listen and read allows students to review the usage of ‘Do you like..?’, ‘I like…’ and short form answers, and the target language from Units 3 and 4, while practising their literacy skills.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful listening, students following the text (with their eyes, or a finger) while they listen, and clear pronunciation, steady pacing and correct identification of each word while they read.
Context
  • “Look, Kate's class are going to the zoo today, too. Let’s listen and read and find out what animals they see and what they like to eat.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. 0
Stage aim
  • Choose the correct answers checks students’ understanding of the text and gives them an opportunity to practise reading for understanding.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct circling/writing of the answers and the ability to identify where in the text they found the answers.
Context
  • “I want to find out more about the elephants. Which one of Kate’s friends should I talk to?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Chant): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video Chant): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations.
