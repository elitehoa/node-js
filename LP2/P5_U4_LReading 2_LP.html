Apollo Lesson Plan

Warm up/Review Game
10
Reader Page 1 - A Before Reading 1. Listen and follow along in your storybook.
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and follow along in your storybook reviews the story by engaging students in listening and text tracking, helping them recall key events and language of the story Echo and Narcissus.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for focused listening, students following along in their books, and correct finger tracking.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read the sentences and choose the correct answers.
0
Stage aim
  • Read the sentences and choose the correct answers allows students to show understanding of story details through multiple choice questions, requiring careful reading and accurate judgment.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate choices and careful reading of sentences.
Reader Page 1 - 3. What do you think Artemis will do?
0
Stage aim
  • This task encourages students to make predictions using story knowledge and personal ideas, supporting comprehension and spoken expression.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for relevant predictions, simple explanations, and confident participation.
Reader Page 1 - B. While Reading 1.a Listen to the two endings
0
Stage aim
  • While Reading engages students at a deeper level by giving them a purpose to read. Listen to the two endings provides two different endings to the story, allowing students to understand how the story changes by listening carefully to the two different endings.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for attentive listening and students recognizing differences between endings.
Reader Page 1 - 1.b Listen and echo.
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and echo models correct pronunciation and intonation, allowing students to practice accurate repetition of key language from the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear echoing, accurate pronunciation, and active oral participation.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read together
0
Stage aim
  • Read together practises oral production and fluency through shared reading, supporting confidence and reinforcing correct rhythm and stress patterns.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct pronunciation, appropriate intonation, and whole-group participation.
Reader Page 1 - 3. Role-play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • Role-play in groups is an opportunity for freer, less-scaffolded language use, encouraging confidence and expressive speaking through character interaction.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic and confident production of the language, turn-taking, and active participation.
Break
0
Reader Page 2 - C. After Reading 1. Read the sentences and choose True or False.
0
Stage aim
  • After Reading allows students to continue working with the language of the story as they review the two different endings of the story and show understanding by answering a series of questions. Read the sentences and choose True or False allows students to show understanding of each ending by selecting correct details from the text.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct True/False choices, careful reading, and students staying on task.
Reader Page 2 - 2. Work in pairs. Complete the worksheet.
0
Stage aim
  • Work in pairs develops understanding of story sequence and cause-and-effect by requiring students to organize events correctly for each ending. Look for logical ordering of events and accurate matching to each ending.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for logical ordering of events and accurate matching to each ending.
Reader Page 2 - 3. Discuss both endings.
0
Stage aim
  • Discuss both endings encourages students to express opinions and preferences using the sentence frame ‘I like Ending 1 because…’.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for use of accurate use of the given sentence frame, clear reasons, and active participation.
Reader Page 2 - Sing along!
0
Stage aim
  • Sing along reinforces comprehension and oral fluency through rhythmic repetition, making language memorable and engaging.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident singing, clear pronunciation, and enthusiastic participation.
