Apollo Lesson Plan

Warm up/Review Game
10
Reader Page 1 - A Before Reading
0
Stage aim
  • Before Reading establishes the context of the story of Arachne and Athena by engaging students with visual clues and guided questions.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate responses, neat handwriting, and participation in discussion.
Reader Page 1 - B. While Reading 1.a Listen and follow along in your storybook.
0
Stage aim
  • While Reading tasks engage students at a deeper level by giving them a purpose to read. Listen and follow provides a model for students, allowing them to hear the natural rhythm and pronunciation of the language.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active listening and engagement, and students following the story in their books with their fingers.
Reader Page 1 - 1.b Listen and echo.
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and echo provides a model for intonation and pronunciation of the language.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct echoing of the language, and active participation.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read together
0
Stage aim
  • Read together practises oral production of the language in the story through chorus reading.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct pronunciation and authentic intonation, and active participation.
Reader Page 1 - 3. Role-play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • Role-play in groups provides an opportunity for freer, less-scaffolded production of the language in the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic and confident production of the language, turn taking and active participation.
Reader Page 1 - 4. Complete the worksheet
0
Stage aim
  • Complete the worksheet checks students’ comprehension of the story and vocabulary through a series of true or false questions.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct answers, neat circling and all students being on-task.
Reader Page 1 - Sing along!
0
Stage aim
  • Sing along further practises oral production of the story and reinforces comprehension.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident singing, correct pronunciation, and active participation.
Break
0
Reader Page 2 - C. After Reading 1. Role play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • After Reading allows students to continue working with the language of the story. Role play in groups provides students with the opportunity to perform together and produce the language of the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident production of the language that conveys an understanding of meaning, turn taking and active participation.
Reader Page 2 - 2. Complete the story map in pairs
0
Stage aim
  • Complete the story map in pairs works on categorising, sequencing and communication skills. It also provides students with the opportunity to speculate on the story by predicting what happens next.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct categorisation and logical predictions that demonstrate comprehension of the story.
