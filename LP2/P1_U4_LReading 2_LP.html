Apollo Lesson Plan

Warm up/Review Game
10
Reader Page 1 - A Before Reading 1. Listen and follow along in your storybook
0
Stage aim
  • Before Reading tasks re-engage students with the language they will meet. Listen and follow along in your storybook re-familiarises students with the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active listening and students following the story in their books with their fingers in synchronicity with the audio and with all students engaged and on-task.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Work in pairs. Choose the correct answer.
0
Stage aim
  • Work in pairs. Choose the correct answer has students discussing what they have understood from listening to the story through guided multiple-choice questions.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for collaboration and communication between students in their pairs, and correct answers which indicate successful comprehension.
Reader Page 1 - 3. What do you think will happen to Red?
0
Stage aim
  • What do you think… allows students to predict the ending, creating deeper engagement and motivation when they read the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active engagement from all students, with all students on-task and making suggestions (which can be in their shared L1).
Reader Page 1 - B While Reading 1a. Listen and follow along in your storybook
0
Stage aim
  • While Reading tasks engage students at a deeper level by giving them a purpose to read. Listen and follow along… re-familiarises students with the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students following the story in their books with their fingers in synchronicity with the audio and all students engaged and on-task.
Reader Page 1 - 1b. Listen and echo
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and echo provides a model for natural intonation and authentic pronunciation of the language in the story which students then produce orally themselves.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic echoing of the language (pronunciation and intonation) and active participation from all students.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read together
0
Stage aim
  • Read together practises oral production of the language in the story through chorus reading aloud.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct pronunciation and authentic intonation and active participation from all students.
Reader Page 1 - 3. Role-play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • Role-play in groups provides an opportunity for freer, less-scaffolded production of the language in the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic and confident production of the language that conveys an understanding of meaning, and all students participating actively.
Break
0
Reader Page 2 - C. After Reading 1. Read and choose the correct answer
0
Stage aim
  • After Reading allows students to continue working with the language of the story whilst developing other process skills. Read and choose the correct answer checks students’ comprehension of the story ending while recycling other language familiar to students.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct choices which students can justify, and neat circling.
Reader Page 2 - 2. Work in pairs. Complete the worksheet.
0
Stage aim
  • Complete the worksheet checks students’ comprehension of the story and vocabulary in a collaborative way, through a series of multiple-choice questions.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students collaborating, correct answers, neat circling and all students being on-task.
Reader Page 2 - 3. Discuss both endings. Which ending do you like best and why?
0
Stage aim
  • The Discuss activity allows students to express a personal opinion about the story, engaging critical thinking skills.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students being engaged and actively communicating their opinions, using English wherever possible supported by their shared L1.
