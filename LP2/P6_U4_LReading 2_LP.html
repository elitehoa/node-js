Apollo Lesson Plan

Warm up/Review Game
10
Reader Page 1 - A Before Reading 1. Listen and follow along in your storybook.
0
Stage aim
  • Before Reading reviews what students have learnt so far, preparing students for the next part of the story. Listen and follow provides a model for students, checking comprehension.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active listening and engagement, and students following the story in their books.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Work in pairs. Match the characters with the correct sentences about them.
0
Stage aim
  • Work in pairs. Match the characters with the correct sentences about them checks understanding of the main points of the story. Students work together to match the characters to descriptive sentences.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for collaboration, team work and correct answers.
Reader Page 1 - 3. What do you think will happen next?
0
Stage aim
  • What do you think will happen next? allows students to predict the next part of the story, fostering deeper engagement and motivation.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active engagement, participation and appropriate predictions based on what they have read.
Reader Page 1 - B. While Reading 1.a Listen to the two endings. Follow along in your storybook.
0
Stage aim
  • While Reading tasks engage students at a deeper level by giving them a purpose to read. Listen and follow provides a model for students, allowing them to hear the natural rhythm and pronunciation of the language. The two endings provide students with a more engaging task and reason to listen.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students following the story in their books with their fingers, and engagement.
Reader Page 1 - 1.b Listen and echo.
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and echo provides a model for intonation and pronunciation of the language.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct echoing of the language, and active participation.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read together
0
Stage aim
  • Read together practises oral production of the language in the story through chorus reading.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct pronunciation and authentic intonation, and active participation.
Reader Page 1 - 3. Role-play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • Role play in groups provides students with the opportunity to perform together and produce the language of the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident production of the language that conveys an understanding of meaning, turn taking and active participation.
Break
0
Reader Page 2 - C. After Reading 1. Read the sentences and choose the correct answer.
0
Stage aim
  • After Reading allows students to continue working with the language of the story. Read the sentences and choose the correct answer checks students’ comprehension of the story ending.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct choices which students can justify, and neat circling.
Reader Page 2 - 2. Work in pairs. Complete the worksheet.
0
Stage aim
  • Complete the worksheet checks students’ comprehension of the story and vocabulary through a series of questions and ordering tasks, while discussing answers with one another..
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct answers, neat lines for matching, and working well with a partner.
Reader Page 2 - 3. Discuss both endings Which ending do you like best? Why?
0
Stage aim
  • Discuss both endings encourages students to collaborate and form opinions and justify, fostering critical thinking skills.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for engagement, participation, forming and giving an opinion and justifying.
