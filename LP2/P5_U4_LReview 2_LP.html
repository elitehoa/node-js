Student Book p.45 - A Listen and write T (True) or F (False).
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and write T (True) or F (False) checks students' recall of the target vocabulary from Units 3 and 4 (foods, taste, habits and spare time activities), and gives them the opportunity to practise listening for detail.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful listening, accurate identification of the target vocabulary, and correct labelling of the boxes with T or F.
Context
  • “I’m confused! We talked about so many different things in the last two units - what people have for lunch, what their character is like and what their hobbies are. But I don’t think all of the pictures match what the children are saying. Can you help me to check? You can? Thank you!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.45 - C. Listen and write.
5
Stage aim
  • Listen and write gives students another opportunity to practise their listening skills, think about appropriate answers to the questions based on the pictures, and practise using the key structures and target language to form complete sentences.
Evidence of learning

Student Book p.46 - E. Choose or write the correct answers. 10m]
0
Stage aim
  • Choose or write the correct answers checks students’ understanding of the text and gives them an opportunity to practise reading for understanding.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct circling/writing of the answers and the ability to identify where in the text they found the answers.
Context
  • “It’s great that the class trip was fun, but I forgot what happened. Who did they speak to? What did they have for lunch? I hope it was nice!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Chant): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video Chant): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4R
