Apollo Lesson Plan

Warm up/Review Game
10
Reader Page 1 - A Review 1. Listen and follow along in your storybook
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and follow along in your storybook re-familiarises students with the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active listening and students following the story in their books with their fingers in synchronicity with the audio and with all students engaged and on-task.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read together
0
Stage aim
  • Read together practises oral production of the language in the story through chorus reading aloud.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct pronunciation and authentic intonation and active participation from all students.
Reader Page 1 - 3. Role play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • Role-play in groups provides an opportunity for freer, less-scaffolded production of the language in the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic and confident production of the language that conveys an understanding of meaning, and all students participating actively.
Reader Page 1 - 4. Match the sentence halves
0
Stage aim
  • Match the sentence halves tests students’ comprehension of the story and their recognition of linguistic elements such as vocabulary, grammar and sentence construction.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct matching, comprehension of the language, neat joining and all students being on-task.
Break
0
Reader Page 1-2 - B Work in pairs. Complete the story map. Use the words in the box.
0
Stage aim
  • Complete the story map. Use the words in the box encourages a deeper reading of the story summary and tests comprehension of the vocabulary in order to apply it at sentence level.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct and logical choices that convey understanding of the language, and neat handwriting.
Reader Page 1-2 - Sing along!
0
Stage aim
  • Sing along reinforces the meaning of the language and provides an extra chance for oral production.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident singing, authentic pronunciation and intonation and all students actively participating.
