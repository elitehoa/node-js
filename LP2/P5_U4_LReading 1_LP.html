Stage aim
  • Before Reading establishes the context of the story Echo and Narcissus by engaging students with visual cues and guided questions to activate background knowledge and encourage prediction.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate responses, relevant vocabulary, and active participation during picture discussion.
Reader Page 1 - B. While Reading 1.a Listen and follow along in your storybook.
0
Stage aim
  • While Reading engages students at a deeper level by giving them a purpose to read. Listen and follow along in your storybook provides a listening model and purpose for reading by allowing students to hear natural rhythm, stress, and pronunciation while tracking the text.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for focused listening, students following along in their books, and correct tracking with fingers.
Reader Page 1 - 1.b Listen and echo.
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and echo models correct pronunciation and intonation, allowing students to practice accurate repetition of key language from the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear echoing, accurate pronunciation, and active oral participation.
Reader Page 1 - 2. Read together
0
Stage aim
  • Read together practises oral production and fluency through shared reading, supporting confidence and reinforcing correct rhythm and stress patterns.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct pronunciation, appropriate intonation, and whole-group participation.
Reader Page 1 - 3. Role-play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • Role-play in groups is an opportunity for freer, less-scaffolded language use, encouraging confidence and expressive speaking through character interaction.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for authentic and confident production of the language, turn-taking, and active participation.
Reader Page 1 - 4. Complete the worksheet
0
Stage aim
  • Complete the worksheet checks students’ comprehension of key story details by requiring them to recall information and select correct answers independently.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate answers, careful reading of questions, neat completion, and students staying focused and on task.
Reader Page 1 - Sing along!

Stage aim

Evidence of learning

Reader Page 2 - C. After Reading 1. Role play in groups
0
Stage aim
  • After Reading allows students to continue working with the language of the story as they review the story and show understanding by talking about what happened and organizing the main ideas in a clear order. Role play in groups provides students with an opportunity to use story language freely and creatively by reenacting scenes, supporting spoken fluency, confidence, and understanding of character roles.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident speaking, appropriate use of story language, turn-taking, and active participation in group interaction.
Reader Page 2 - 2. Complete the story map in pairs
0
Stage aim
  • Complete the story map in pairs checks students’ understanding of story structure, characters, setting, and key events by organizing information from the text into a beginning–middle–ending framework.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate completion of details, correct use of vocabulary from the word bank, cooperative pair work, and students staying on task.
