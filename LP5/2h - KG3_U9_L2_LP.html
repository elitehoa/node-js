Student Book p36 - Point and say
0
Stage aim
  • Point and say sets up the context for the story by drawing on visual elements students are familiar with and initiates some oral communication.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct answers to the teacher’s questions, active participation and engagement from all students.
Context
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p36 - Listen to the conversation. Act it out.
0
Stage aim
  • Listen to the conversation presents the target language in context, reinforced by the pictures, checking students’ ability to recognise the spoken and written forms. Act it out checks understanding of the target language and provides controlled practice of oral production.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active engagement, students being on-task and following the story through correct pointing, then pointing at the correct speech bubbles. For Act it out, look for accurate and confident repetition, active participation, correct pronunciation and accurate intonation.
Context
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
