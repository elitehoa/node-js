Student Book p.27 - A Listen. Then act it out.
15
Stage aim
  • Listen. Then act it out consolidates the unit's vocabulary and grammar within a narrative context. Students listen to understand the story, then role-play the dialogue to practise fluency and intonation.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for attentive listening to the story, confident delivery of the dialogue during the role-play and appropriate use of expressions and gestures.
Context
  • “Ben is joining our class today and Minh is showing him around. Let’s find out what she shows him.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Follow): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video, then act it out): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.28 - Tongue Twisters Listen and say
10
Stage aim
  • Tongue twisters provide a fun and challenging focus on pronunciation. Students listen to and repeat the sentences, practising clear articulation of the target long vowel sounds (o_e and u_e).
Evidence of learning
  • Look for enthusiastic repetition and clear, accurate pronunciation of the target phonics sounds within the tongue twisters, even when said quickly.
Context
  • “Great work showing the new kids around! Now let’s get them helping us with something really tricky - tongue twisters!”
