Student Book p.20 - A Look and learn
10
Stage aim
  • Look and learn introduces students to forming reported speech by reviewing changes in pronouns, tense, and sentence structure when retelling information.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students identifying how direct speech changes in the examples, noticing grammar shifts, and explaining the pattern correctly.
Context
  • “I have lots of friends, but they don’t all know each other. I have to tell them what everyone else wants to do! It’s not easy, but I can help them arrange something fun for the weekend. Do you know how to tell people what someone said?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

5

Student Book p.20 - C Read the sentences and complete the dialogues.
10
