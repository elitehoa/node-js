Student Book p.32 - A Look & learn
15
Stage aim
  • Look and learn presents the grammar rule for using conjunctions of time. The table provides clear examples of how 'when', 'before', 'after', and 'until' are used to connect clauses.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students studying the table and being able to explain the time relationship each conjunction creates (e.g., 'What happens first if we use before?').
Context
  • “When I was young, I had long hair. Before I went to school, I brushed it for one hour! After school, I brushed it again. In fact, until I was twelve years old, I didn’t cut my hair once! Do you believe me? To make our stories more interesting, we need to connect our ideas in time. And there are some great words to help us!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p.32 - B Circle the correct words.

Stage aim

Evidence of learning

Student Book p.32 - C Choose and complete the sentences.
0
Stage aim
  • Choose and complete the sentences provides productive practice in forming complex sentences. Students must match a main clause with a subordinate time clause from the box to create a logical sentence.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly completed sentences where the chosen time clause logically connects to the main clause.
Context
