Student Book p.34 - A Look & learn
15
Stage aim
  • Look and learn presents the grammar rules for 'have to'. The table clearly contrasts the positive form for obligation with the negative form for a lack of obligation, providing clear examples.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students studying the table and being able to explain the difference in meaning between 'have to' and 'don't have to'.
Context
  • “Every night I have to sleep. It’s very important! But in the morning I don’t have to eat breakfast. I can have a big lunch! We've been talking a lot about things we have to do, and don’t have to do. Can you remember when to use it? Let’s have a look and find out!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p.34 - B Complete the sentences using have/has to.
20
Stage aim
  • Complete the sentences provides controlled practice of the target grammar. Students must complete the sentences with the correct positive or negative form of 'have to', paying attention to subject-verb agreement.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly completed sentences that use the appropriate positive or negative form and the correct subject-verb agreement ('have to' vs. 'has to').
Context
  • “Let's practise using this new grammar. I've started some sentences, but the most important part is missing. Can you use the clues to complete each sentence? How fast can you do it? Good luck!”

Student Book p.34 - C Circle the correct words.
0
Stage aim
  • Circle the correct words assesses understanding of the grammar in context. Students must read each situation, understand the logic, and then circle the correct form to complete the sentence meaningfully.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly circled words in all six sentences, showing students can apply the grammar rules based on logical understanding of the context.
Context
  • “And now your final challenge! It’s all about logic. I’m hungry, so I have to… do what? That’s right, eat! My food is too hot, so I (don’t have to!) heat it. Easy, right? Use your grammar knowledge to choose the right option! Let’s go!”
Procedure
