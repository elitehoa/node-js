Student Book p.15 - A Listen and say. Then practise.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say introduces the new target vocabulary. Listen and say gives students an opportunity to practise saying the target words. Then practise introduces the lessons key structure ‘that’ to talk about things that are far away.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful listening, clear sounds, and confident repetition.
Context
  • “I’ve got my invisible friend Martha here today. Where she’s from, schools look a little different. Can you help her learn what we have in our classroom?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.16 - Speaking Guessing game Put the cards face down. Pick a card and ask. Then guess and answer.
30
Stage aim
  • The guessing game provides the opportunity to further use the target vocabulary and structure in a more engaging way.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct placement of cards (‘this’ cards closer than ‘that’ cards) and mostly correct use of ‘What’s this/that…?’
Context
  • “She’s just told me, she’s really confused about when we use ‘this’ and ‘that’. Can you play this game to help her understand?.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (closed pairs): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (open pairs): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p.16 - Song
10
Stage aim
  • The song strengthens retention of the target language and helps improve pronunciation.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for most students participating and able to join in with at least the most repeated parts of the song and/or do the actions to demonstrate their understanding.
Context
  • “Martha is really grateful for you all teaching her so much! Let’s sing a song together that she can share with her friends back home.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Sing): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video Song): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
