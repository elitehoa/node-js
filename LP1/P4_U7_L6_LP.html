Student Book p.32 - A Look & learn
15
Stage aim
  • Look and learn presents a clear, visual overview of the modal verb 'should'. The table demonstrates the positive and negative forms, highlighting that the structure does not change for different pronouns.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students focusing on the grammar table and their ability to answer simple check questions about the positive and negative forms.
Context
  • “I think I should eat burgers every day. No? I shouldn’t?! OK, then I should stay in bed every day! No?! OK I need to learn more about using ‘should’ for advice. Let’s practise and say it perfectly every time!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.32 - C Look at the list and complete the sentences with should or shouldn't.
10
Stage aim
  • Look at the list... provides further contextual practice of 'should' and 'shouldn't'. Students read a list of rules and use that information to complete sentences about what someone should or shouldn't do.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly completed sentences that accurately reflect the information given in "Mike's Home Rules", using the correct form of 'should'.
Context
  • “Do you have rules in your house? In my house, I should take my shoes off, and I shouldn’t eat in bed! Not everyone is good with house rules though. Can you tell me what Mike should and shouldn't do? Excellent! Thank you!”
