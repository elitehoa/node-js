Student Book p.29 - A Look & write
10
Stage aim
  • Look and write serves as a comprehensive review of the unit's language. Students must identify health problems and then write the corresponding logical advice, consolidating vocabulary and grammar.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for the correct identification and spelling of the illnesses, and the correct and logical advice written for each child.
Context
  • “Today, we’re not in class. We’re in the hospital clinic! And I’m the doctor. We have three new patients today. Can you help me? I need to know what’s the matter, and then we can choose what to do!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.87 - A Trace and write.

Student Book p.30 - Speaking Doctor and Patient Game
30
Stage aim
  • The Doctor and Patient Game is a culminating speaking task that synthesizes the unit's language in a communicative role-play. Students interact freely to match problems with advice, encouraging fluent language use.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active participation, confident use of the full dialogue, and students correctly following the game's rules of matching and switching cards when they find the right 'doctor'.
Context
  • “Our classroom is now a big hospital! Half of you are doctors and half are patients. Patients, you must find a doctor who can help you. Doctors, you must give the right advice. The first person to switch all their cards is the winner!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
