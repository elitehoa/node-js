Student Book p.29 - A Look and write using the words in the box.
10
Stage aim
  • Look and write... serves as a comprehensive review of the unit's quantifiers and container phrases. Students must analyse each picture and select the most logical phrase from the word bank to write in the blank.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly written phrases in all six blanks, demonstrating that students can logically match the quantifier to the visual representation of the food.
Context
  • “This is our final picnic, and the table is almost set! Before we start, I need your help to write the correct labels for all the food. Please choose the best phrase from the box for each dish.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.30 - Presentation My Sandwich Recipe
30
Stage aim
  • My Sandwich Recipe is a culminating project that synthesizes the unit's language. Using a scaffolded template, students create and write a personalized recipe, which they then present to the class.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly completed recipes in both steps. During the presentation, listen for clear pronunciation, logical sequencing of the recipe steps, and confident delivery.
Context
  • “Today, we are all going to be chefs, and our special task is to create a brand new sandwich recipe! First, let's complete this tuna sandwich recipe together as practice. Then, you will create your own amazing sandwich and present the recipe to the class like a famous TV chef!”
