Student Book p1-2 - A Listen and say
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and say checks understanding of everyday classroom instructions and the teacher’s language. Say practises first-step pronunciation of common classroom instructions.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful listening, correct pairing of instructions to pictures, clear pronunciation of phrases such as raise your hand and confident repetition.
Context
  • “Today, we’re going to make some classroom rules! Let’s listen to this teacher to give us some ideas.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Break [10m] - C. Listen and say. Then review your own classroom rules.
0
Stage aim
  • Listen and say checks understanding of classroom rules and phrases, linking spoken language to the behaviours shown in each picture. Review your own classroom rules checks students’ ability to personalise the target language, and apply the phrases given to their own classroom context.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate naming of rules, correct stress and intonation of key phrases, students referring to the poster when speaking, and simple classroom rules that reflect the classroom context.
Context
  • “Let’s listen to these rules, and then we can agree our own! Think about which ones you like and which ones you want to change.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
