Student Book p.31 - A Complete the menu.
10
Stage aim
  • Complete the menu serves as a comprehensive review of the unit's vocabulary. Students must analyse the visual clues on the menu, including pictures and symbols, to select and write the correct word in each blank.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for a correctly completed menu, with all blank spaces filled with the logical word from the word bank based on the visual evidence provided.
Context
  • “My friend is opening a new restaurant, but the menu isn't finished yet! They have the pictures and prices, but some words are missing. Can you use the clues and the word bank to help me complete it?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.32 - Presentation Be a Food Critic!
30
Stage aim
  • Be a Food Critic! is a culminating project that synthesizes the unit's language. Students use a scaffolded process to choose, rate, and write a review of a restaurant meal, culminating in a presentation to the class.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for a logically completed chart and a coherent written blog post. During the presentation, listen for clear pronunciation and confident delivery of their review.
Context
  • “Today, you all have a very important job because you are going to be famous food critics! Your mission is to choose one of these restaurants, write a full review in your blog, and then present your expert opinion to the class.”
