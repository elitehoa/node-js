Student Book p.15 - A Listen and say. Then practise.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and Say checks understanding of word meaning and pronunciation of the phrases shown, then practise.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful listening, clear pronunciation of words and phrases, correct intonation and use of the visual and written prompts provided.
Context
  • “This party will be fun! Look! Someone is blowing up balloons and someone else is setting the table…if we listen carefully, we can find out who is doing what job. Thank you for your help!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.16 - Speaking Some information is missing from the party checklist! Ask for it and write the names

Stage aim
  • Missing information gives pupils a chance to review what they have learnt against the information they’ve been given.
Evidence of learning

Procedure

Heads Down (closed pairs): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Student Book p.16 - Complete the email from Rosa
10
Stage aim
  • Complete the email allows students to apply target structures in a written context. It reinforces recall and written accuracy.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for logical answers that fit the context, appropriate vocabulary, accurate spelling.
Context
  • “Rosa didn’t finish her email about the party! Let’s help her complete the message so she can send it to her friends. Can you work out what her friends are doing for the party?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Sing): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video Song): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
