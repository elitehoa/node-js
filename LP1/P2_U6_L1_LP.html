Student Book p.11 - A Listen, point and say Audio track 76
10
Stage aim
  • Listen, point and say checks understanding of word meaning and sound. Listen and point shows students can match what they hear to the right image. Say practises the first-step pronunciation.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful listening, correct pointing, clear pronunciation of words and confident repetition.
Context
  • “Shall we start a class band? I play the drums. What instruments could you learn to play?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book P.12 P 45 - Chant Audio track 80
10
Stage aim
  • Read, listen and say provides the opportunity to use the target structure and vocabulary in a chant as well as practising reading and listening skill.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate reading as well as good pronunciation as students follow the lyrics.
Context
  • “I can’t wait for our first rehearsal! We’re going to be the best band ever!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Chant): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video Chant): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4R
