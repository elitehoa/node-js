Student Book p.29 - A Look & write
10
Stage aim
  • Look and write serves as a comprehensive review of the unit's language. Students must analyse the posts to connect the events with the resulting feelings and complete the sentences correctly.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly completed sentences in all four posts, showing that students can logically connect the described events and the corresponding feelings.
Context
  • “Many people like to post online about their day! Not me, I only tell my friends. For example, I’ve got lots of work to do tonight. Can you guess how I feel? That’s right! I feel anxious. Let’s see if you know how these people are feeling”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.30 - Presentation Silly Sentences
30
Stage aim
  • Silly Sentences is a culminating project that synthesizes the unit's language in a creative game. Students generate random combinations of events and feelings, record them, and present the most humorous results.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active and engaged participation in the game, a correctly filled chart, and a clear, confident presentation of the silly sentences to the class (and a few laughs!).
Context
  • “Do you think I’m silly? I hope not! But today I will be! Because now we're going to play a game called 'Silly Sentences'! You’ll use your cards to play with your group to make funny combinations. Then you can present the silly sentences your to the class. Let’s see who gets the silliest sentences!”
