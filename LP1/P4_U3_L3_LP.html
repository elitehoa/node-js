Student Book p.29 - A Listen. Then act it out.
15
Stage aim
  • Listen. Then act it out consolidates the unit language within a narrative context. Students listen to understand the story's plot and character motivations, then role-play the dialogue to practise fluency and intonation.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for attentive listening, understanding of the plot twist (the surprise), and confident delivery of the dialogue with appropriate emotion and intonation.
Context
  • “Do you like surprises? I do! Do you want to read about a special surprise for Minh? Let's listen to her conversation to find out what the surprise is!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Follow): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video, then act it out): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.83 - A Read, write, and number.
10
Stage aim
  • Read, write, and number consolidates narrative comprehension skills. Students must use context clues to complete the dialogue with the correct words and then number the story frames in the correct logical sequence.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurately completed sentences using the word bank and correct numbering of the story frames from 1 to 6.
Context

Student Book p.30 - Tongue Twisters Listen & say
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say provides focused pronunciation practice on the consonant blends 'st' and 'sm'. The tongue twister format encourages students to articulate the sounds clearly and at speed.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear and accurate articulation of the 'st' and 'sm' consonant blends, even when students attempt to say the sentences quickly.
Context
  • “Now for a fun challenge! Let's try to say some tricky tongue twisters. Listen carefully to the 'st' and 'sm' sounds, then see how fast you can say them without getting your tongue in a twist!”
Procedure
