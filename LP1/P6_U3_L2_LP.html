Student Book p.27 - A Listen and say. Then practise.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say introduces new vocabulary for talking about fun activities. Then practise provides initial practice of the grammar structure used to ask and answer questions about personal preferences.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear pronunciation of the six activity phrases and accurate repetition of the target question and 'I like to...' answer structure.
Context
  • “After a long week of school and chores, it's important to have fun! I often chat online with my friends or watch cartoons. What about you? What do you like to do in your spare time? Let’s look at some ideas!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.28 - Speaking What Have They Got in Common?
30
Stage aim
  • What Have They Got in Common? is a culminating speaking task that synthesizes the lesson's language. Students use a scaffolded process to find information, complete an information-gap task, and compare results.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident interaction during the information-gap task, correctly ticked boxes, and a well-formed written sentence that accurately compares the two characters' preferences.
Context
  • “In my room, I have a computer, a TV and lots of books. Can you guess what I like to do in my spare time? That’s right! But can you find out what Kevin and Rosa have in common? I need you to be great detectives and find out!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (closed pairs): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (open pairs): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
