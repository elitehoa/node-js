Student Book p.13 - A Listen and say. Then practise.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say checks understanding of word meaning and pronunciation of activity phrases. Practise allows students to listen and repeat the phrases in a structured question and answer.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful listening, clear pronunciation and repetition of the target phrases, accurate sound-image recognition, and confident pronunciation.
Context
  • “I asked my friends about their weekend plans, they told me lots of things but I can’t remember everything. I need to practise listening! Let’s listen to what the children said… that will help! Can you tell me what they said?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.14 - Speaking Board Game
30
Stage aim
  • Board game provides freer speaking practice with the reported-speech pattern as students move across the board, identify activities from pictures, and produce personalised ‘He/She said that…’ sentences.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active participation in the game, students using “What did he/she say?” and “He/She said that…” without much prompting, accurate use of activity phrases, and natural turn-taking.
Context
  • “Let’s play a game to find out what the children did on the weekend. There are lots of cool activities. I want to do them too! Are you ready? Who is going to be the winner? Do you think you can beat me?! Let’s go!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (closed pairs): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (open pairs): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
