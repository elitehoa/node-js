Student Book p.33 - A Listen and read
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and read presents some real-world content related to the topic of animals and recycles language students are familiar with.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful reading and check that students are on task.
Context
  • “My ears are blocked today - I’m going to need all your ears to listen for me! Let’s listen to find out more about animals and what they eat?
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Phonics Book P.117 p.118 - A Listen and say
5
Stage aim
  • Listen and say makes students aware of the difference between the short /ʊ/ and the long /uː/ sounds.
  • Listen and say situates the target sounds within the whole word and trains students to recognise the combination of the ‘oo’ sound (/ʊ/ and /uː/) and the consonant which follows.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for attentive listening and correct production of each sound.
  • Look for accurate pronunciation and all students being on task.
Context
  • “English words are very strange! Look at this!” (Hold up book flashcard.) “What is it? Yes, it’s a book! Oo, oo, book!” (pronounce each ‘oo’ as /ʊ/). And look at this! (Hold up balloon flashcard.) What is it? Yes, it’s a balloon! Oo, oo, balloon! (pronounce each ‘oo’ as /uː/). Can your amazing ears hear the difference? Let’s learn about more oo (/ʊ/) and oo (/uː/) sounds!
  • “Let’s play a listening game! Which word sounds like ‘moon’? Is it ‘school’? Or is it ‘balloon’? Yes, it’s balloon! Let’s listen for some more words that sound similar!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
