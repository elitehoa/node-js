Student Book p.27 - A Listen and say. Then practise.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say introduces the target vocabulary through audio modeling and repetition. Then practise provides initial controlled practice of the new question and answer structure using the new words.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear pronunciation of the new food words and accurate use of ‘Have you got any…?’ during the practice phase.
Context
  • “I’m having a BBQ! What do I need?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.28 - Speaking Sandwich Bingo
30
Stage aim
  • Sandwich Bingo motivates students to use the target language through gamification. Students create a personalised board and practise asking ‘Have you got any...?’ to achieve the winning pattern.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active interaction between pairs, accurate question formation, clear pronunciation, and correct identification of the winning 'X' pattern.
Context
  • “Time to BBQ! Let’s make our burgers. Yummy!”
Procedure

Heads Down (closed pairs): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (open pairs): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p.28 - Song
10
Stage aim
  • The song consolidates target vocabulary and grammar through melody and repetition. It allows students to practise natural intonation in a musical context.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for enthusiastic participation, clear pronunciation of the target questions and polite responses, and correct rhythm and melody from the whole class.
Context
  • “Those burgers were so good! Let’s have another one soon!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Sing): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video Song): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
