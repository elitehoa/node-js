Student Book p.29 - A Listen and read
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and read consolidates the unit's language within a narrative context. Students read and listen to understand the plot, follow the dialogue, and comprehend the story's humorous conclusion, as well as learn new vocabulary.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students actively following the text, showing understanding of the dialogue through their reactions, and laughing or smiling at the final picture and punchline.
Context
  • “Do you want to go to a buffet? What do you want to eat? Let’s get some ideas! Nick and Rosa went to a buffet at the weekend, but I think Nick was much hungrier than Rosa! Let's read to find out what happened.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Follow): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video, then act it out): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p.29 - B. Choose or write the correct answers.
10
Stage aim
  • Choose or write the correct answers checks students' comprehension of the story. The mixed-format questions require them to recall key details about what the characters ate and how the food tasted.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for the correct picture choice for question 1, the correct adjective choice for question 2, and the correctly written word ("everything") for question 3.
Context
  • “That was a funny story! But I can’t remember everything that happened. Who had the tuna? Did Nick have fruit salad? Let’s see if you can help me!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Activity Book p.85 - A Read and number.
10
Stage aim
  • Read and number checks students' understanding of narrative structure and sequencing. Students must read the jumbled parts of the story and number them in the correct chronological order.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for the correct sequence of numbers (1-4) written in the boxes, demonstrating that students have understood the logical flow of the story.
Context

Procedure

Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
Activity Book p.85 - B Read and circle T (True) or F (False).
5
Stage aim
  • Read and circle T or F assesses reading for specific detail. Students must read each statement carefully and decide if it is true or false based on the information given in the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurately circled answers (T or F) for all four statements, showing a clear comprehension of the story's key facts.
Context
  • “Thank you for fixing the story! Now, I've written down some facts about it, but I'm not sure if they are all correct. Could you be a fact-checker and circle True or False for me?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.

Student Book p.30 - D. Make Your Own Story Presentation
20
Stage aim
  • The Presentation serves as a culminating, productive task for the unit. Students first use a template to write a personalized dialogue, then present their story to the class to demonstrate fluency and command of the language.
Evidence of learning

Context
