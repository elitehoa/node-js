Student Book p.17 - A. Complete the travel brochure.
10
Stage aim
  • Complete the travel brochure helps students to develop their reading and writing skills through using simple pictures and maps to create a travel brochure. It helps build accuracy with vocabulary for attractions and directions.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct attraction names, clear and simple direction sentences, accurate use of map clues, and neat handwriting.
Context
  • “Do you think I could be a tour guide? I love talking and writing about famous places! What about you? Could you create your very own brochure? Then let’s vote for our favourite place to visit!
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.18 - Speaking Look at the places to find. Ask & answer with your friend and complete the map.
30
Stage aim
  • Look at the places to find helps students develop their map-reading skills by asking them to find places on the map. Ask and answer helps students with their speaking and listening skills and builds confidence and oral fluency.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for creativity, good use of travel vocabulary, accurate responses to the prompts on the map (travel times etc), and students taking turns asking questions and answering them.
Context
  • “Wow, what great work today! You’ve remembered where to go and how to get there. Now I really want a holiday! Let’s play a Speaking Game to celebrate. Tell your friend what’s on your map. Lead the way!”
