Student Book p.27 - A Listen and say. Then practise.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say introduces new adjectives for describing taste. Then practise provides controlled practice of the Past Simple structure for asking and answering questions about how food tasted.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear pronunciation of the six taste adjectives and accurate repetition of the target question and answer structures.
Context
  • “I had some sweets this morning. But, they weren’t sweet - they were sour! They’re really popular. Do you like them? Let's learn some new words to describe flavours, then we can practise asking how food tastes!
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.28 - Speaking Board Game
30
Stage aim
  • The Board Game provides a fun, communicative context for fluency practice. Students use the game to ask and answer questions about how food tasted, consolidating the lesson's grammar and vocabulary.
Evidence of learning
