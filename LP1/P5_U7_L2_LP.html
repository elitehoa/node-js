Student Book p.25 - A Listen and say. Then practise.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say introduces quantifiers used with countable and uncountable nouns. Then practise provides initial practice of the question forms 'How many...?' and 'How much...?' with their corresponding answers.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear pronunciation of the quantifier phrases and accurate repetition of the 'How many/much...?' questions and 'There is/are...' answers.
Context
  • “Hooray, we've finally arrived at our picnic! Now that all the food is laid out, let's learn how to talk about the quantity of everything. We need to use different words for things like cherries and juice.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.83 - A Match and write.
10
Stage aim
  • Match and write consolidates the use of quantifiers. Students must match the correct quantifier to each food item and write the full phrase, reinforcing the rules for countable and uncountable nouns.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurately written phrases that correctly pair the quantifier with the food item and match the corresponding picture.
Context
  • “It's time to check all the food we brought for our picnic. The labels are all mixed up! Can you help me match the right quantity word to the right food, and then write the full label for each dish?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.

Student Book p.26 - Speaking What Did You Bring on the Picnic?
30
Stage aim
  • This Speaking activity is a culminating speaking task that synthesizes the lesson's language. Students use a scaffolded process of personalization, information exchange, and presentation to talk about picnic foods.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident interaction during the pair work, an accurately completed chart based on the dialogue, and a clear, audible presentation about their friend's picnic foods.
Context
  • “It's time to pack our own picnic baskets! First, choose four of your favourite foods and stick them in your basket. Then, you'll be a reporter: interview your friend to find out what they have, complete the chart, and present your findings to the class.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (closed pairs): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (open pairs): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
