Student Book p.25 - A Listen and say. Then practise.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say introduces new adjectives for describing feelings. Then practise provides initial practice of the grammar structure used to ask and answer questions about how someone felt when something happened.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear pronunciation of the six feeling adjectives and accurate repetition of the target question and 'I felt...' answer structure.
Context
  • “Last week I argued with my friend. Do you remember? Can you guess how I felt? That’s right… I was very upset. But then I won my football game! And I felt really confident. How about you? Can you talk about how things made you feel? Let’s try!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.87 - A Circle and write.
5
Stage aim
  • Circle and write consolidates the spelling of the new feeling adjectives. Students use the picture clues to find and circle the correct word in a letter string, then write it correctly.
Evidence of learning

Context

Student Book p.26 - Speaking Board Game
30
Stage aim
  • The Board Game provides a fun, communicative context for fluency practice. The game encourages students to spontaneously connect past events with feelings, consolidating the lesson's grammar and vocabulary.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active participation from all students, correct use of the question and answer structures, and clear pronunciation of the feeling adjectives and past events.
Context
  • “You know I love playing games. Do you want to play a game too? Great! We can use the pictures to talk about our feelings and what happened. Keep an eye out for the bonus car to jump ahead! The first person to reach the finish line is the winner. Good luck!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (closed pairs): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (open pairs): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
