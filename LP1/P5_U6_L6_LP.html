Student Book p.20 - A Look & learn
10
Stage aim
  • Look & learn helps students recognise key vocabulary and sentence structures by providing examples of positive and negative imperatives and preparing them for writing and speaking activities.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for good understanding of the grammar shown in the table and evidence of understanding key structures.
Context
  • “Remember! It’s very important to follow the rules in different countries. Can you think of some rules here? When I’m on holiday, I am always polite! And I’m never rude. Can you help me practise correct sentences for instructions? Let’s be nice and make friends on holiday!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
