Student Book p.27 - A Listen. Then act it out.
15
Stage aim
  • Listen. Then act it out consolidates the unit language within a narrative context. Students listen to understand the story's plot and humorous twist, then role-play the dialogue to practise fluency and intonation.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for attentive listening, understanding of the humorous ending, and confident, expressive delivery of the dialogue when acting out the story.
Context
  • “Oh no! Minh isn’t feeling well. She’s not at school today. Can you guess what’s the matter? No? Do you want to read and find out? Great!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Follow): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video, then act it out): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.85 - A Read, write, and number.

Student Book p.28 - Tongue Twisters Listen & say
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say provides focused pronunciation practice on the vowel sounds ‘oo’ and ‘ew’ (/ʊ/ vs. /uː/). The tongue twister format encourages students to clearly differentiate and articulate the two similar sounds.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear and accurate articulation of the vowel sounds, with a noticeable difference in pronunciation between words like 'looked' (/ʊ/) and 'flew' (/uː/).
Context
  • “Let's have some fun with two tricky sounds! The sounds in 'book' and 'flew' are very similar. Listen carefully, then let's try to say these tongue twisters without getting them mixed up!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
