Student Book p.27 - A Listen and read.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and read consolidates the unit's language within a narrative context. Students read and listen to understand the plot, follow the dialogue, and comprehend the story's humorous conclusion, as well as learn new words.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students actively following the text as they listen, and watch for their reactions (smiles or laughter) to the funny ending when Tim reveals what he brought.
Context
  • “Let's join our friends Minh, Rosa, and Tim on their picnic! They have all brought their own picnic baskets, and I'm very curious to see what's inside each one. Let's listen and read to find out what they all brought for lunch.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Follow): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video, then act it out): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.85 - A Read and correct a - e.
10
Stage aim
  • Read and correct develops proofreading skills and reinforces grammar. Students must carefully read the text to find and correct five specific errors related to quantifiers and plural forms.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for the five corrected phrases written accurately on the lines below the text.
Context
  • “I was writing down the picnic story, but I think I made some grammar mistakes. I need your expert help! Can you please read my story carefully, find the five errors, and write the corrections for me?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
Activity Book p.85 - B Read and circle T (True) or F (False).
5
Stage aim
  • Read and circle T or F assesses reading for specific detail. Students must read each statement and decide if it is true or false based on a careful reading of the corrected story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurately circled answers (T or F) for all four statements, showing a clear comprehension of the story's facts.
Context
  • “Thank you for correcting my story! Now that it's perfect, let's do a final fact-check. Please read these statements and circle True or False to confirm all the details are correct.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.

Student Book p.28 - C Imagine you and your friend are on a picnic. Write and share.
10
Stage aim
  • Write and share provides a free-production task for the lesson. Students use the prompts to write about an imagined picnic, then share their ideas with a partner to exchange information.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for logically completed written answers that use the target vocabulary and quantifiers correctly. Listen for confident sharing and interaction between partners using “How many/much...?.”
Context
  • “We've read about Tim's funny picnic. Now, let's imagine our own! First, write down what you brought. Then, interview your friend to find out what's in their basket, because good friends always share their food at a picnic!”
Procedure

Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Student Book p.28 - Presentation
30
Stage aim
  • The Presentation serves as a culminating, productive task for the unit. Students use a template to write a personalized version of the picnic story, then present it to the class to demonstrate their command of the language.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for a logically and grammatically completed story that uses the unit's language correctly, and listen for a clear, confident, and audible presentation delivered to the class.
Context
  • “For our presentation, you get to be the star of the picnic story! Your mission is to use this template to write your own version, including your friend's name and the food you brought. Afterwards, you will present your story to the class.”
Procedure
