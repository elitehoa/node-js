Student Book p.29 - A Listen and read.
15
Stage aim
  • Listen and read consolidates the unit's language in a narrative context. Students read a dialogue that contrasts preferences with obligations, reinforcing the different grammatical structures in a realistic scenario, as well as learn new vocabulary.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students actively following the text and demonstrating understanding of the dialogue and the reason why Kevin cannot play with his friends.
Context
  • “I never know what to do after school. There are so many fun hobbies! Do you make plans with your friends after school? Rose, Kevin and Minh have lots of great ideas for fun activities, but there's a problem: let’s read the story and find out what it is!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Follow): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video, then act it out): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.87 - A Read and number.
10
Stage aim
  • Read and number checks students' understanding of narrative structure and sequencing. Students must read the jumbled parts of the story and number them in the correct chronological order to reconstruct the dialogue.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for the correct sequence of numbers (1-4) written in the boxes, demonstrating that students have understood the logical flow of the conversation.
Context
  • “The story of the friends' after-school plans has been all mixed up! The conversation doesn't make sense in this order. Can you please read the parts carefully and number them correctly to fix the dialogue?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
Activity Book p.87 - B Read and circle T (True) or F (False).
0
Stage aim
  • Read and circle T or F assesses reading for specific detail and inference. Students must read each statement and decide if it is true or false based on the information and implications in the story.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurately circled answers (T or F) for all four statements, showing a clear comprehension of the story's facts and implications.
Context
