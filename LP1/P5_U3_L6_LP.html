Student Book p.34 - A Look & learn
15
Stage aim
  • Look and learn presents the grammar rule for using adjectives after linking verbs. The table clearly lists the target verbs and provides example sentences to show the correct sentence structure.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students paying attention to the grammar table and being able to identify a linking verb when asked by the teacher.
Context
  • “How many senses do we have? Can you guess? Taste, look, sound, feel, smell… we have lots of ways to talk about how things are. But do you know how to say them correctly? Let’s practise!””
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p.34 - B Underline the linking verbs and circle the adjectives.
20
Stage aim
  • Underline... and circle... provides controlled practice in identifying the new grammar pattern. Students must analyse each sentence to find both the linking verb and the adjective that follows it.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly underlined linking verbs and correctly circled adjectives in all six sentences.
Context
  • “Now that we know the rule, let's practise being grammar detectives. I need your help finding the adjective and linking verb! Let’s see how fast you can do it! Ready? Go!”
Procedure

Student Book p.34 - C Look at the pictures and complete the sentences.
15
Stage aim
  • Look at the pictures and complete the sentences provides productive practice of the grammar structure. Students must select an appropriate noun and adjective from the word bank to complete each sentence logically.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for logically and grammatically correct sentences, with the correct noun and adjective chosen to match each picture.
Context
  • “Great work finding those words! That was the easy bit. Can you do the same thing for pictures? And then complete a sentence about them? Hmm I think you can!”
Procedure
