Student Book p.31 - A Look & write
10
Stage aim
  • Look and Write serves as a comprehensive review of the unit's vocabulary. Students must identify the six different after-school activities shown in the picture and write the correct phrases to consolidate their spelling.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for the six vocabulary phrases written correctly and spelled accurately in the corresponding blanks on the page.
Context
  • “Imagine someone looking into your house after school - what would they see? Is someone watching cartoons? Or maybe feeding a dog? Families do lots of different things! Let’s have a look and see what we can see… .”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.32 - Speaking Let's Do a Survey!
30
Stage aim
  • Let's Do a Survey! is a culminating speaking and data-handling task. Students use the unit's key questions to interview their classmates, gather real information, and represent their findings on a graph.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for confident interaction and questioning between students during the survey. The main evidence is an accurately completed graph that visually represents the data they collected.
Context
  • “Today, you are all going to be researchers! Your mission is to conduct a survey to find out what is most popular in our class. First, choose your own answers. Then, interview your friends, and record the information on your graphs. Can you guess what’s the most popular activity? I bet I can!”
Procedure
