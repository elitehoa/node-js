Student Book p.19 - A Choose & write
10
Stage aim
  • Choose and write helps students select the correct word for each sentence and write it clearly. It supports vocabulary recognition, spelling, and neat handwriting.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct word choices, logical matching for each sentence, and accurate spelling.
Context
  • “You know, I think we can make our party even bigger! What kind of food do you want? How many decorations do we need? And of course we need lots of activities… Let’s put the words in the right place and make this party really amazing! We can think of extra things too!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.20 - Presentation Plan a birthday party for Bruno. Decide what you and the animal friends will do. Make an invitation. Complete your invitation and present it to your class.
30
Stage aim
  • Planning a birthday party helps students use creative language to make simple decisions about food, games, and activities. Making an invitation supports writing short, meaningful sentences with party vocabulary. Presenting an invitation to the class builds speaking confidence and lets students share their ideas using clear, simple English.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear, simple ideas about the party, correct and contextual sentences on the invitation and confidence when presenting to the class, as well as clear communication.
Context
  • Sing: Happy birthday to Bruno! “Let’s make sure Bruno has the best birthday ever. How old do you think he is? Can you help the animals plan the fun and design his party invitation? Tell the class so Bruno can hear how much fun his party will be!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
