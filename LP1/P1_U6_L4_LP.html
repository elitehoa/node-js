Student Book p.17 - A Look and write
10
Stage aim
  • Look and write helps students to review how to spell some of the unit’s key words.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct identification of the objects in the picture, and correct spelling.
Context
  • “I’m opening a shop selling picnic food! I need your help to write labels with the names of the food I’m going to sell.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.18 - Speaking Stick and complete your picture. Ask and answer. Then stick.
35
Stage aim
  • Stick and complete your picture. Ask and answer. Then stick. helps students practise their speaking skills in relation to the target language.This strengthens fluency, and gives them an opportunity to demonstrate turn taking skills.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students correctly identifying the objects in the picture, accurately describing where they are, accurately placing the stickers on the second picture based on their classmate’s description, and natural turn-taking with classmates.
Context
  • “Great work, now we’re nearly ready to open the shop! You can be my first customers. Where will you put the food you buy?.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books.
