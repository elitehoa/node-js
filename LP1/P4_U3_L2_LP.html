Student Book p.27 - A Listen and say. Then practise.
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and say introduces new verb phrases for daily activities. Then practise provides initial controlled practice of the past simple tense, including the question form and both regular and irregular verbs.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear pronunciation of the new activity phrases and accurate repetition of the question "What did you do yesterday?" with the correct past tense answers.
Context
  • “Yesterday, I went to the supermarket. What did I buy? Yes! I bought some fruit! We know where everyone was yesterday, but what did they do there? Let's learn some new words for activities, then we can practise asking about our day.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.81 - A Cross out, look, and write.
5
Stage aim
  • Cross out, look, and write consolidates vocabulary spelling with a puzzle activity. Students must identify and remove extra letters to reveal the correct verb before writing the full activity phrase.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly crossed-out letters, accurate identification of the verbs, and correctly spelled verb phrases on the lines.
Context
  • “Oh dear, a word puzzle! There are extra letters hiding in all these words. Can you be a detective, cross out the wrong letters, and write the correct activity phrases? How fast can you do it?”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.

Student Book p.28 - Speaking Do All the Activities Spin, ask and answer. Then tick.
30
Stage aim
  • Do All the Activities provides a fun, communicative context for fluency practice. Students use the board game to ask and answer questions about past activities, consolidating the unit's grammar and vocabulary.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active participation, correct use of the question and answer structures, clear pronunciation, and students correctly following the rules of the game.
Context
  • “Now it's time for a game! Let's use the spinner to travel around the town. When you land on a picture, ask your friend what they did yesterday, and tick the box. Tick all the places to win! Good luck!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (closed pairs): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (open pairs): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p.28 - Song
10
Stage aim
  • The Song consolidates the grammar for asking about likes (Do/Does... like to...) through melody and repetition. It allows students to practise natural intonation for questions and answers in a fun, musical context.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for enthusiastic participation, clear pronunciation of the questions and answers, and a correct sense of rhythm and melody from the whole class.
Context
  • “That was a fun game! To finish our lesson, let's sing a song about all the different activities that people like to do.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Sing): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video Song): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
