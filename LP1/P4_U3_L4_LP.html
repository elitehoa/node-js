Student Book p.31 - A Look & write
10
Stage aim
  • Look and write serves as a comprehensive review of the unit's language. Students must use the visual and text clues to write the correct past tense activity phrases, consolidating vocabulary and grammar.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly written past tense phrases that logically fit the context of each sentence and picture.
Context
  • “Do you know what I did last week? No? I got a haircut! What do you think? Let's see what these friends did last weekend. Look at the pictures and help me complete the sentences.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p.32 - Speaking Our Silly Day! Ask & answer
30
Stage aim
  • Our Silly Day! is a culminating speaking task that synthesizes the unit's language in a creative, communicative game. Students combine places and actions to create humorous scenarios, encouraging fluent use of the target grammar.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for active and enthusiastic participation in the group discussion, confident use of the question and answer forms, and successful completion of the writing task based on their friends' silly answers.
Context
  • “Let's imagine yesterday was a very silly day! I think I watched a film at the supermarket. That's silly! Let's play a game to create our own silly day stories and find out who had the silliest day of all.”
