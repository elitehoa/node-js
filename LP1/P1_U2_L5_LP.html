Student Book p.21 - A Listen and read
15
Stage aim
  • Listen and read allows students to review the usage of ‘this’ and ‘that’ and practise their literacy skills.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students following the text (with their eyes, or a finger) while they listen, and clear pronunciation, steady pacing, and correct identification of each word while they read.
Context
  • “When I was a kid, my classroom looked like this! Listen and read to find out what things are.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Phonics Book pp. 109 - 111 - A Match
10
Stage aim
  • Match strengthens first-sound awareness by having students identify the initial phoneme, connect it to the correct letter, and demonstrate early phonics control by linking each sound to a picture that begins with it.
  • Match further develops first sound identification and an awareness of word families.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate pronunciation of the sound, correct identification of the pictures and correct isolation of their first sound.
  • Look for correct identification of the words which share the same middle and last sounds, and neat lines to connect them.
Context
  • “I learnt the same sounds you are learning now! What sounds do these letters make? Please say them for me!”
  • “I always had balloons at my birthday parties. Can you help me match the balloons that have the same vowel and final consonant sounds.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
