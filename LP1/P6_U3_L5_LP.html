Student Book p.33 - A Listen & read
10
Stage aim
  • Listen and read develops reading comprehension through a cross-curricular text focused on data. Students read an informational article to understand statistics about routines and learn new academic vocabulary.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students actively following the text, showing an ability to locate the specific numbers and percentages as they are mentioned in the reading.
Context
  • “When I was young, I was very lucky. After school I played sports every day. And then I did my homework… and after dinner I watched TV! Do you want to know what children in the UK do after school? Do you think it’s the same as you? Let’s find out!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p.33 - B. Answer the questions.
15
Stage aim
  • Answer the questions assesses comprehension of the data-rich text. The questions require students to find specific statistics (percentages and minutes) and perform a simple calculation based on the reading.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct multiple-choice selections and the accurately calculated number in the final question, showing comprehension of the text's data.
Context
  • “Excellent reading, researchers! But I can't remember all those numbers. Can you? What do most students do when they come home after school? Do they go to sleep? No? Let’s see how you do with these questions!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
