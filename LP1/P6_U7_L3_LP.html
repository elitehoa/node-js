Student Book p.27 - A Listen and read.
15
Stage aim
  • Listen and read consolidates the unit's language within a narrative context. Students read a dialogue where characters discuss past events and their resulting feelings, reinforcing the key grammar and vocabulary.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students actively following the text and demonstrating understanding of the different events and feelings experienced by each of the three characters.
Context
  • “How many good things happened to you today? Do you often tell your friends about things that happened? I do! It’s important to tell friends how we feel. Let’s listen to Amy, Kevin and Rosa talking about their day”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Follow): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video, then act it out): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.89 - A Read and number.
10
Stage aim
  • Read and number checks students' understanding of narrative structure and sequencing. Students must read the jumbled parts of the story and number them in the correct chronological order to reconstruct the dialogue.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for the correct sequence of numbers (1 to 4) written in the boxes, demonstrating that students have understood the logical flow of the conversation.
Context
  • “The conversation between our friends here has been mixed up and is in the wrong order. Please read the different parts of the dialogue and number them correctly so the story makes sense.”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.

Student Book p.28 - C Think about what happened at school. Write and share.
20
Stage aim
  • Write and share provides a personalized production task. Students reflect on experiences that caused certain feelings, write about them using the target language, and then share their stories with a partner.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for logically completed written answers that use the Simple Past to describe an event. During the sharing phase, listen for confident and supportive interaction between partners.
Context
  • “Can you think of some good things and some bad things that happened to you at school? How did you feel? Write down your ideas, and then share your stories with a partner. I’d love to hear them too!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Act it out): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
