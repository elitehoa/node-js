Student Book p.15-16 - A Listen. Then act it out.
20
Stage aim
  • Listen. Then act it out reinforces understanding of target language in a story using visuals as an aid. Students act out the story to practise using comparatives in context.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students carefully listening to the story and engaging with the visuals, then correct imitation of the pronunciation and intonation of the lines, with appropriate gestures.
Context
  • “Tim and his dad are looking at holidays! Let’s listen to the story and find out where they decide to go!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up (Reading): Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and help Ss to understand the text by asking questions, practicing skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific information) and detail (reading for comprehension). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down (Listen and Follow): Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together (Video, then act it out): Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p.73 - A Read, write, and number

Stage aim
  • Read, write, and number tests reading comprehension and reinforces language structures by asking students to identify the correct word to go in each gap and resequence the scenes from a story into the correct order.
