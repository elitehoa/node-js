Student Book p.34 - A Look and learn.
10
Stage aim
  • Look and learn presents a comprehensive overview of the Past Simple of 'be'. The table clearly structures the positive, negative, and question forms for all pronouns, serving as a key reference.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for students actively studying the table and correctly identifying the right verb form for a given pronoun (e.g., 'What do we use for They?').
Context
  • “What did I do on Saturday? Was I at school? No! I was at home! Were you at the library yesterday? You were? Great! There’s lots of ways to use ‘was’ and ‘were’. Now let's look at them together and grammar experts!”
Procedure
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p.34 - B. Read and circle.
10
Stage aim
  • Read and circle provides controlled practice of the Past Simple of 'be'. Students must choose the correct verb form (was/were) to agree with the subject of each sentence.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for the accurate circling of was or were (and their negative forms) to match the subject in each of the four sentences.
Context
  • “Okay, experts, let's test our new knowledge. I'm not sure which word is correct in these sentences. Can you read them and circle the right one for me?”
Procedure

Student Book p.34 - C Look and write.
15
Stage aim
  • Look and write provides productive practice of the grammar. Students use the picture prompts to write full questions and then provide personalised short answers, consolidating the complete structure.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correctly formed questions using the right verb and pronoun, and logical, personalised short answers in the correct format.
Context
  • “Now, let's write some questions about these pictures. After you write the question, I want you to give your own true answer. Were you at the park yesterday?”
