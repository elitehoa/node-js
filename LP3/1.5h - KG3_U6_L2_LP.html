Student Book p10 - Point and say.
0
Stage aim
  • Point and say strengthens recognition of target words with word-picture matching, reinforcing whole-word memory.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate pointing to the pictures, correct identification of the target language and clear pronunciation.
Context
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs
Student Book p10 - Listen to the conversation. Act it out.
0
Stage aim
  • Listen to the conversation exposes students to the structures What are you wearing? and Are you wearing…? and reviews the target language from the previous lesson. Act it out allows students to practise using the target language in a natural way and to practise working with a partner.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for careful listening and accurate pointing to the items in the picture as students hear them when listening to the conversation. Look for accurate pronunciation, stress, intonation, and participation from both students in each pair when acting it out.

Student Book p10 - Read and trace your answer.
0
Stage aim
  • Read and trace strengthens recognition of short-form answers to questions using the key structure Are you wearing…? and adds a degree of personalisation to the task. It reinforces letter order, and neat handwriting.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct identification of the answer that is true for them, correct pencil grip, consistent letter formation, and neat handwriting within the lines.
