Student Book p23-24 - Point & say
0
Stage aim
  • Point and say gives students controlled practice of the target language and helps them progress from saying single words to linking the target language and key structures to make simple sentences.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate pronunciation of the target language and correct identification of the target vocabulary (what’s this? Big / small)..
Context
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Activity Book p49 - Give the children their toys. Cut and stick.
0
Stage aim
  • Give the children their toys allows students to be creative within a clear context. Cut allows students to practise their fine motor skills. Stick also aids fine motor skills, improving hand-eye co-ordination and developing precision.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for correct handling of scissors and careful cutting out, an appropriate amount of glue used and the ability to direct the glue where intended, as well as each pictured child being ‘given’ toys in line with the instructions.
Context

Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction.
