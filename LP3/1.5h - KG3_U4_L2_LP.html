Student Book p30 - Point and say.
0
Stage aim
  • Point and say activates vocabulary and prepares students for the story dialogue. Students identify the characters and the food options on the table before listening.
Evidence of learning
  • Look for accurate pointing to the specific plates (chicken, pizza, fish) and naming the items.
Context
  • Heads Up: Focus the students’ attention on the CPT and present relevant features of the language (MFP). Then demonstrate the activity with TA/students. Heads Down: Ss open their books. Instruct and ask ICQs. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Heads Together: Demo/Instruct and ask ICQs, including setting behaviour expectations. Then monitor, providing support and stretch where needed. Conduct activity feedback and error correction. Reward moment Check behaviour expectations and reward with APOS or remind for 4Rs

Student Book p30 - Sing the song
0
Stage aim
  • Sing the song consolidates the dialogue structure and social manners. Students practice the full exchange of offering ("Would you like?"), politely declining ("No, thank you"), and stating a preference ("I'd like...") with the magic word "please".
Evidence of learning
  • Look for clear pronunciation of the rejection "No, thank you" and the use of "please" at the end of the request, staying in time with the rhythm.
